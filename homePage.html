Celeste Speedrunning

Celeste, an indie 2D platformer that was initially created for the Pico-8, a modern retro-console, is one of the most popular video games to speedrun. Since, the game's initial release in early 2018, speedrunners have brought the time to beat the game down to just over 25 minutes.

Speedrun Categories

As it is such a popular speedgame, being the fifth most run game on speedrun.com, there are many categories of runs that players can submit. The three main categories are Any%, where you unlock the Epilogue from a new file, All Red Berries, where you collect all 175 of the optional red berries and complete the level "Core" from a new file, and True Ending, where you complete the epilogue level "Farewell" from a new file.

While none of these categories are by any means easy to become a skilled player at, the easiest is Any%, as you have to learn and practice the least amount of the game.

Timing

Celeste has an in-game timer that can be activated in the game's settings, which is used to time speedruns. Optional external timing programs, such as livesplit, are used to compare times of individual segments between multiple runs. These timing programs can use a custom plugin (which can be found in the external resources page) in order to read the time from the game to help the runner focus on playing. With this plugin installed, timing will automatically be paused between levels.

Any% Route

The different types of levels in Celeste are named after the sides of a casette tape. The main levels that are unlocked by playing through the story are called A-Sides, and are the easiest to complete, the secondary levels that are unlock by collecting a cassette hidden in the main level are called B-Sides, while the tertiary levels that are unlocked after beating every A-Side and B-Side in the game are called C-Sides. C-Sides are the most challenging levels in the game, but are not played in the three main speedrun categories.

Any% Level Order

The Any% route follows the game's main level progression throughout most of the run, however, it deviates slightly in the fifth level, the Mirror Temple. Due to the length of the Mirror Temple, it's actually faster to spend a few minutes to collect the cassette tape that unlocks the B-Side of Mirror Temple, return to the map, and play through the B-Side.

Because you can unlock the next level by beating the B-Side instead of the A-Side, in the early days of the category, many runners used more than one B-Side to beat the game. However, other optimizations made every B-side except for Mirror Temple's obsolete in the Any% run, which is only still used due to the large number of skips that are possible on the stage. Due to its unique nature, Mirror Temple B-Side has its own page on this site.
