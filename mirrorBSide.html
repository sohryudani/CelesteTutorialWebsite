Mirror Temple B-Side Route

Mirror Temple B-Side is the only humanly viable B-Side in the current Any% run of Celeste. Mirror Temple B-Side is in a unique position because of many glitches and tricks that allow for a majority of the first section of the stage to be skipped.

Skipping the Keys

There are three keys in the second section of Mirror Temple B-Side, and thanks to many tricks, all three can be skipped, making the B-side faster than the A-Side.

Bubsdrop's Usage

As mentioned on the Glitches and Exploits page, Bubsdrop is only utilized on Mirror Temple B-Side. The trick is utilized in the room directly to the right of the first checkpoint. You are intended to take the vertical exit at the right side of the room, then proceed to complete the room it leads to. However, if you perform a Bubsdrop, fall back into the room, pause the game, and click retry, Madeline will respawn on the platform at the right side of the room, skipping three other rooms and saving over 20 seconds.

This respawn exploitation works because most rooms in Celeste only have two respawn placements, one at the left and one at the right side of the room. Depending on where you enter the room, Madeline will respawn at one of these two points. The room where Bubsdrop is performed only has two respawn points, so if you enter the room from anywhere on the right half, Madeline will respawn at the room's rightmost exit. This accounts for the first keyskip.

Keyskip Two

Two rooms after Bubsdrop is performed, there is a long narrow spike passage that is meant to use a key to pass through. However, right before the spike passage, there is just enough room to perform an extended reverse-hyper. This hyper stays low enough to avoid all of the spikes, and gives you just enough distance to skip the need for the second key in the level.

Keyskip Three

The final keyskip uses another exploit covered on the Glitches and Exploits page, spikejumps. In the room immediatly after the second keyskip, there is a vertical column of spikes that is too tall to pass through with a dash. You are intended to collect a key to get passed this room, however, a spikejump allows you to avoid this key. You can spikejump off of the right wall of the column and dash upwards to skip the key.

This trick will require lots of practice, as this is the hardest spikejump in the game. Due to how narrow the column is, you must perfom a neutral spikejump, combining a neutral jump as introduced in the Basic Movement Tips page, and a spkiejump, to reduce the horizontal distance the spike jump gives while maintaining the height gained.

After the Keys

From here on out in the level, it is just required that you complete the rest of the level well to save time from playing through the A-Side. This youtube video by Programmatic can be followed to learn the optimal methods to complete the rest of the rooms in the level.
